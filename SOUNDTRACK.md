# SOUNDTRACK.md — Claude Art Online

Audio design spec for the 5 floors. Everything here is **instrumental**, meant to
be generated in Suno and dropped into `public/assets/audio/`. The Suno prompts are
copy-paste ready.

Global reference: **Yuki Kajiura** (the real SAO composer). Hybrid orchestra +
electronica, piano ostinatos, layered strings, wordless choir (vocalise as an
instrument — no lyrics). Guiding tone from `VISION.md`: **wonder first, danger
underneath** — every cue is bittersweet, never purely epic or purely serene.

---

## The recurring theme — "The Ascension Motif"

The project-specific signature: **one 4-note melody** that reappears on every floor,
transformed. It's the theme of the lone swordsman climbing the tower.

**The motif (in A minor):**

```
 A  →  E  →  F  →  E
 1     5    ♭6    5
 (▲ perfect 5th leap)  (▲ step)  (▼ fall back)
```

- The **ascending perfect-5th leap** (A→E) = aspiration, the climb, the wonder.
- The step to the **♭6 (F)** = the ache / the looming threat — the note that "burns".
- The fall back to **E** = unresolved, suspended. It never comes home — until Floor 5.

Rhythm: **long – long – short – long**, with a light pickup. Cantabile, memorable in 2s.

**Two sonic signatures in EVERY track (the glue):**

1. **The Tower drone** — a very high held note / shimmer that stays above the music
   the whole time. It's the tower, always visible in the sky. The constant "ceiling".
2. **The Gate toll** — a single low resonant hit (gong / prepared piano) that closes
   every exploration track. Each floor ends at a sealed gate.

**Emotional arc of the motif across the 5 floors:**
Floor 1 states it pure and nostalgic → Floor 2 freezes and fragments it → Floor 3
makes it tragic-heroic → Floor 4 buries it in the deep register → Floor 5 finally
**resolves** it into a full major-key anthem. The suspended leap only comes home at the top.

**Production order:** make **Floor 1** first (states the pure theme), then **Floor 5**
(the resolution), then fill 2-3-4 in between. Use each track as Suno
reference/extend so the melody carries across.

## Floor 1 — Verdant Town · *Town of Beginnings*

Green-gold village, saturated blue sky, soft edge-hiding fog. Church, tavern, market,
windmill, banners. Home — the calm before the climb, but still a prison.

**Mood:** nostalgic wonder, gentle hope, fragile safety.
**Style:** pastoral folk-orchestral, warm cozy fantasy town theme.
**Instruments:** fingerpicked acoustic guitar, harp, warm strings, tin whistle/recorder,
light hand percussion, discreet glockenspiel.
**Tempo/key:** ~78 BPM, A minor slipping to C major (bittersweet).
**Motif:** stated **pure and complete** for the first time, on tin whistle then strings. Innocent — the mother theme.

> `instrumental pastoral fantasy town theme, warm acoustic guitar and harp, gentle strings, tin whistle melody, nostalgic bittersweet, cozy medieval village, soft glockenspiel, 78 bpm, A minor to C major, wordless choir pad in the distance, hopeful yet melancholic, Yuki Kajiura style, high sustained shimmer note held throughout`

---

## Floor 2 — Frostbound · *The Frozen Wastes*

Pale frost-blue snowfield, falling snow, dead/cut trees, ice ruins (a demolished siege
catapult). Silence, desolation, dead beauty.

**Mood:** glacial emptiness, isolation.
**Style:** rarefied cinematic ambient, glacial dark-ambient, minimal.
**Instruments:** glassy pads, celesta / bowed vibraphone, spaced reverberant piano,
breathy flute, very distant wordless choir, sub-bass drone.
**Tempo/key:** ~60 BPM (or pulseless), A minor, long silences.
**Motif:** **fragmented and frozen** — only the first two notes (A→E), very high on ice
bells (celesta), dying before they complete. The melody "broken by the cold".

> `instrumental glacial dark ambient, sparse frozen soundscape, glassy pads, bowed vibraphone and celesta, distant reverberant piano, breathy flute, faraway wordless choir, deep sub-bass drone, 60 bpm, A minor, desolate lonely snowfield, icy bell fragments, slow and haunting, lots of silence, high sustained shimmer note`

---

## Floor 3 — Storm Peaks · *The Howling Reach*

Sunset highlands, orange sky, low blazing sun, purple-shadowed rock, sweeping wind (no
storm — wind only). Fallen bastion ruins, remains of a lost battle. Peak of the first three.

**Mood:** epic-melancholic, dramatic, embers of something lost.
**Style:** epic cinematic orchestral, windswept, tragic-heroic.
**Instruments:** full strings, taiko / low toms, horn swells, a lamenting ethnic wind
(duduk or erhu), epic wordless choir.
**Tempo/key:** ~90 BPM building, A minor / dorian, taiko drive.
**Motif:** **heroic-tragic and complete**, carried by horns and strings at full width —
the emotional peak of the original trilogy. The ♭6 (F) really hurts here.

> `instrumental epic cinematic orchestral, tragic heroic, full sweeping strings, french horn swells, taiko and low toms, mournful duduk solo, wordless epic choir, windswept sunset battlefield, 90 bpm building, A minor dorian, sorrowful yet grand, dramatic crescendo, Yuki Kajiura style, high sustained shimmer, ends on a deep resonant gong toll`

---

## Floor 4 — The Elderwood · *Mistfen Hollow*

Deep ancient forest, thick close dark-green fog (claustrophobic), a valley crossed by an
old river, pollen motes, an overgrown woodcutter's camp, mist. Primeval, sacred-eerie.

**Mood:** mystery, ancient sacredness, something sleeping that watches you.
**Style:** organic mystical folk-ambient, primeval forest, ritual.
**Instruments:** handpan / hang drum, low flutes and pan flute, prepared piano,
plucked kora/harp, deep cello, flowing-water & nature textures, deep drone, very low wordless choir.
**Tempo/key:** ~66 BPM flowing (like the river), dorian/phrygian modal.
**Motif:** **hidden in the low register**, on cello or bass flute — barely recognizable,
as if the ancient wood half-remembers the tune. Buried under textures.

> `instrumental mystical forest ambient, primeval and sacred, handpan and hang drum, low bass flute and pan flute, prepared piano, plucked kora, deep cello, flowing water and nature textures, low wordless choir drone, 66 bpm, dorian phrygian modal, eerie and ancient, misty enclosed woodland, hidden melody in the low register, meditative and unsettling, high sustained shimmer`

---

## Floor 5 — Craghold · *Castle Rock*

Edinburgh-inspired high medieval city, castle on a rock crag, Royal Mile / Grassmarket
spine lined with banners, bright blue sky, wide horizon. Civilization, majesty, the
summit-city: the arrival.

**Mood:** grandeur, ceremony, bittersweet triumph, the top of the climb.
**Style:** grand Celtic/Scottish orchestral, processional, ceremonial.
**Instruments:** Highland/uilleann pipes, bodhrán, fiddle, Celtic harp, full orchestra,
brass fanfare, imposing wordless choir.
**Tempo/key:** ~100 BPM, stately processional march, A major / mixolydian (finally major).
**Motif:** **resolved into a full major-key anthem** — the same 4 notes, but the
suspended leap finally comes home, richly harmonized with fanfare. The payoff of the
whole climb. Triumphant, with a vein of cost underneath.

> `instrumental grand Celtic Scottish orchestral, ceremonial processional, Highland bagpipes and uilleann pipes, bodhrán, fiddle, Celtic harp, full orchestra brass fanfare, triumphant wordless choir, 100 bpm stately march, A major mixolydian, majestic hilltop castle city, epic homecoming, the main theme finally resolved and harmonized in major, bittersweet victory, high sustained shimmer, ends on a deep resonant gong toll`

## Boss Gate cues — "the motif, weaponized"

Five short stingers (**30–40s**), one per floor. Same instrumentation as the floor, but
**darkened**, and the 4-note theme **twisted into menace**: the 5th leap becomes a
**tritone**, the ♭6 drags into dissonance, tempo doubles. Structure of each cue:

**Gate toll (gong) → tower drone bending dissonant → distorted motif erupts → assault
percussion → hard cutoff (the gate breaks open).**

Shared boss signature: a **dissonant rising swell** = the attack telegraph (the golem
eye lighting up). The opening gong is the *same* toll that closes the exploration track —
the gate that ends a floor is literally the note that opens its boss.

- **Floor 1 — Gate of Beginnings:** `instrumental boss battle intro sting, 40 seconds, dark orchestral, low aggressive strings, heavy war drum, the gentle folk theme twisted into menace, tritone intervals, dissonant rising swell, 140 bpm, A minor, ominous, gong hit at start, sharp cutoff ending`
- **Floor 2 — The Frozen Gate:** `instrumental frozen boss sting, 35 seconds, dark glacial orchestral, distorted icy bells, pulsing sub-bass, low growling wordless choir, brittle percussion, the frozen melody bent into a tritone, cold and threatening, 130 bpm, A minor, gong opening, abrupt icy cutoff`
- **Floor 3 — The Fallen Bastion Gate:** `instrumental epic boss battle sting, 40 seconds, aggressive cinematic orchestral, pounding taiko, snarling brass, screaming duduk, full choir shout, tragic heroic theme weaponized into a tritone, windswept fury, 150 bpm building, A minor dorian, gong slam, dramatic hard stop`
- **Floor 4 — The Old Gate:** `instrumental primeval boss sting, 35 seconds, dark ritual ambient turning aggressive, pounding handpan, shrieking low flutes, aggressive cello ostinato, churning water texture, deep choir drone, ancient melody twisted dissonant, 135 bpm, phrygian, gong toll, sudden cutoff`
- **Floor 5 — Castle Rock Gate:** `instrumental Celtic war boss sting, 40 seconds, aggressive Scottish battle orchestral, war bagpipes, pounding bodhrán, brass fanfare collapsing into minor, tribal choir, the triumphant anthem corrupted into a tritone, 145 bpm, A minor, gong opening, epic hard stop`

## Victory sting (optional, 10–15s)

Played when a gate opens and you climb: the motif resolving to major.
> `instrumental short victory fanfare, 12 seconds, the main 4-note theme resolving triumphantly into major, warm brass and strings, wordless choir swell, bright bells, hopeful uplifting, a gate opening, gentle bittersweet undertone`
